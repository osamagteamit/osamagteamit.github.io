Dubai

The UAE is home to records: From the largest and tallest illuminated building facade, the Burj Khalifa, to the largest water fountain in the world, and the largest mosaic panel in the world, the UAE continues to beat records. Abu Dhabi is home to the largest handwoven carpet in the world, on a huge area of ​​5,630 square metres. The size of the carpet was woven into nine parts, and when it was ready, the total area inside the Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque was assembled

Dubai has the largest flower garden in the world, which is known as the Miracle Garden. This masterpiece occupies more than 1.5 million square feet and attracts more than 20,000 visitors daily.
The Capital Gate building in the Emirate of Abu Dhabi leans more than the Leaning Tower of Pisa, and has been designed to tilt 18 degrees towards the west.
Dubai Police proudly use supercars to patrol the streets of Dubai, such as Ferrari, Bentley and Lamborghini, so it is surprising to see the fastest police car in service, the Bugatti Veyron.
Dubai is known as the City of Gold, as it has a major business center for the sale of gold, as the Gold Souk there is a huge maze of stalls selling as much gold as people are looking for.
The UAE is a popular country for tourism and vacationers. Every year more than 12 million tourists visit the UAE, with tourism now making up more than 10% of the country's GDP.
There are no permanent rivers in the United Arab Emirates, due to its unique location within the Arabian Desert. .
